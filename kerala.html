Temples
Sabarimala Temple

Sabarimala Temple is one of the most famous pilgrimage destinations in Kerala. This temple is located in the Western Ghats of Pathanamthitta district and is dedicated to Lord Ayyappan. Every year, millions of devotees undertake a rigorous 41-day vratham before visiting, culminating in the climb of the sacred 18 steps known as Pathinettampadi.

Padmanabhaswamy Temple

Padmanabhaswamy Temple is a revered and ancient Vishnu temple in Thiruvananthapuram. This temple is famous for its Dravidian architecture and the reclining form of Lord Vishnu on the serpent Ananta. It is considered one of the 108 Divya Desams and is also known for the mysterious underground vaults and immense treasures.

Aryankavu Sastha Temple

Aryankavu Temple is a prominent shrine dedicated to Lord Shasta in Kerala’s Kollam district. This temple uniquely depicts the deity in a youthful Brahmachari form and follows Tamil and Kerala traditions. The temple's architecture and rituals reflect the cultural blend of both regions, especially during the Mandala season.

Location favorite Wishlist
Kulathupuzha Sastha Temple

Kulathupuzha Temple is another major temple dedicated to Lord Shasta, situated near Punalur. Here, the deity is worshipped in the form of a child, known affectionately as Bala Sastha. Surrounded by dense forests and riverbanks, the temple exudes divine calmness and attracts pilgrims throughout the year.

Location favorite Wishlist
Achankovil Sastha Temple

Achankovil Temple is one of the five sacred Shasta temples, located in the forests of Kollam district. Here, the deity is depicted in a Grihastha (householder) form along with his consorts, Poorna and Pushkala. The temple is famous for its medicinal offerings and is deeply connected to traditional healing beliefs

Sree Poornathrayeesa Temple

Sree Poornathrayeesa Temple is located in Tripunithura and is dedicated to Lord Vishnu in the form of Santhana Gopala Moorthy. This temple is well known for its grand festivals, including Vrischikotsavam, which draws thousands of devotees. The deity here is believed to bless childless couples and is deeply revered in the region.

Location favorite Wishlist
Chottanikkara Temple

Chottanikkara Bhagavathy Temple is a powerful and vibrant shrine near Ernakulam. The goddess here is worshipped in three forms—Saraswati in the morning, Lakshmi at noon, and Durga in the evening. It is also renowned for healing mental ailments and exorcisms through divine rituals.

Location favorite Wishlist
Guruvayoor Temple

Guruvayoor Temple is one of the most visited temples in Kerala, situated in Thrissur district. Dedicated to Lord Krishna as Guruvayurappan, it is called the "Dwarka of the South." Known for its strict rituals and the grandeur of Krishna Janmashtami celebrations, it draws lakhs of devotees annually.

Location favorite Wishlist
Ernakulathappan Temple

Ernakulathappan Temple is a revered Shiva temple located in the heart of Ernakulam city. The temple complex also houses shrines for Parvati and Ganesha and showcases classic Kerala architecture. It plays a key role during the Utsavam festival and is a peaceful spiritual center amidst the city bustle.

Location favorite Wishlist
Vaikom Mahadeva Temple

Vaikom Mahadeva Temple is one of the oldest and most important Shiva temples in Kerala, located in Kottayam district. This temple is part of the prominent "108 Shiva temples" and is associated with the legendary Vaikom Satyagraha. Devotees believe a visit here grants peace, strength, and liberation from obstacles in life.

Historical Places
Bekal Fort

Bekal Fort is one of the largest and most well-preserved forts in Kerala, located along the Arabian Sea coast in Kasaragod. This 17th-century fort offers breathtaking sea views and holds historical importance due to its strategic role in coastal defense. Its unique architecture and surrounding beach make it a prominent heritage and tourist site.

Location favorite Wishlist
Fort Kochi

Fort Kochi is a charming coastal area in Ernakulam known for its colonial history and Portuguese, Dutch, and British influences. The iconic Chinese fishing nets, St. Francis Church, and colonial buildings reflect its diverse past. It remains one of Kerala's top heritage zones with art cafés, museums, and historic lanes.

Location favorite Wishlist
Padmanabhapuram Palace

Padmanabhapuram Palace is a magnificent wooden palace located in Thuckalay near Thiruvananthapuram. It is an architectural marvel built in the traditional Kerala style and served as the seat of the Travancore royal family. Intricately carved interiors, murals, and antiques highlight its cultural grandeur.

Location favorite Wishlist
Thalassery Fort

Thalassery Fort, situated in Kannur, is a reminder of Kerala’s colonial past under British rule. Built in the 18th century, it served as a military center and trade hub during British expansion. Its sturdy laterite walls, secret tunnels, and location near the sea enhance its historical appeal.

Location favorite Wishlist
Hill Palace

Hill Palace is the largest archaeological museum in Kerala, located in Tripunithura, Kochi. It was once the residence of the Kochi royal family and now houses royal artifacts, coins, and ancient manuscripts. Set amidst landscaped gardens, it offers insights into Kerala’s regal heritage.

Location favorite Wishlist
Palakkad Fort

Palakkad Fort is a historic fort built by Hyder Ali in the 18th century in the heart of Palakkad town. Later taken over by the British, the fort witnessed many battles including those involving Tipu Sultan. Surrounded by a moat and greenery, it stands as a powerful symbol of resistance.

Location favorite Wishlist
Krishnapuram Palace

Krishnapuram Palace, located in Kayamkulam, is famed for its traditional Kerala architecture and mural art. The palace was built by Marthanda Varma and features the famous "Gajendra Moksham" mural. It also houses a museum showcasing artifacts and sculptures of historical significance.

Location favorite Wishlist
Mattancherry Palace (Dutch Palace)

Mattancherry Palace in Kochi, also called the Dutch Palace, was originally built by the Portuguese. Later renovated by the Dutch, the palace features Kerala murals, portraits of Kochi rulers, and royal regalia. It stands as a fusion of European and Kerala design influences.

Location favorite Wishlist
St. Francis CSI Church

St. Francis Church, located in Fort Kochi, is one of the oldest European churches in India, dating back to the 16th century. It is historically significant as the original burial site of Portuguese explorer Vasco da Gama. The church’s simple yet elegant architecture reflects early colonial craftsmanship.

Location favorite Wishlist
Edakkal Caves

Edakkal Caves in Wayanad are ancient rock shelters featuring carvings and inscriptions that date back to the Neolithic age. These caves are located on Ambukuthi Hills and require a trek to reach. The site is a treasure trove for history enthusiasts and a symbol of Kerala’s prehistoric past.

Waterfalls
Vazhachal Falls

Vazhachal Falls is a scenic waterfall located in Kerala’s Thrissur district. It features a wide, cascading flow surrounded by lush forests near Athirappilly. The area is rich in biodiversity and is a peaceful spot for nature lovers.

Soochipara Fallsur

Soochipara Falls is a three-tiered waterfall located in Wayanad, Kerala. It drops from about 200 meters and is surrounded by dense forest. A short trek leads to the falls, where visitors can swim and enjoy nature.

Palaruvi Falls

Palaruvi Falls, meaning "stream of milk," is located in Aryankavu, Kollam, Kerala. It cascades from a height of 300 feet through lush, green forest surroundings. The natural pool below is popular for bathing and is said to have healing properties.

Location favorite Wishlist
Athirappilly Water Falls

Athirappilly Falls is Kerala’s largest waterfall, dropping 80 feet over the Chalakudy River. It lies near the Sholayar forest and is surrounded by rich wildlife and greenery. Famous as a film location, it’s most stunning during the monsoon season.

Location favorite Wishlist
Kallar Meenmutty Waterfalls

Kallar Meenmutty Falls is located near Ponmudi in Kerala. Reaching the falls involves a peaceful trek through dense forest. The clear waters and natural surroundings attract many visitors.

Location favorite Wishlist
Lakkam Waterfalls

Lakkam Waterfalls is located near Marayoor inside Eravikulam National Park. It flows down from the Eravikulam Plateau into a natural pool surrounded by forest. Visitors enjoy trekking, boulder climbing, and bathing in the cool waters.

Location favorite Wishlist
Marmala waterfall

Marmala Waterfalls is located near Teekoy in Kottayam, Kerala. It drops about 60 meters into a deep emerald pool below. Visitors reach it after a 2 km trek through a rubber estate.

Location favorite Wishlist
Makkiyad Meenmutty Water Falls

Makkiyad Meenmutty Falls is a three-tiered waterfall in Wayanad, Kerala. It’s reached by a short walk through tea plantations and greenery. The clear waters and peaceful setting attract many nature lovers.

Location favorite Wishlist
Adyanpara Falls

Adyanpara Falls is a seasonal waterfall near Nilambur, Kerala. It’s known for its clean, medicinal waters and forest surroundings. The area is popular for swimming, picnics, and nearby attractions.

Location favorite Wishlist
Kumbhavurutty Waterfalls

Kumbhavurutty Falls is a two-tier waterfall near Aryankavu, Kerala. It drops about 25 meters into a natural pool surrounded by forest. Visitors enjoy trekking and spotting wildlife in the nearby area.
