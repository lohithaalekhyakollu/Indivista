INDIVISTA

State: Kerala, India

Temples
Sabarimala Sree Ayyappa Temple

One of the most famous pilgrimage sites in India, Sabarimala temple is dedicated to Lord Ayyappa. Located amidst the Western Ghats in Pathanamthitta district, it's accessible only after a rigorous trek through dense forests. The temple attracts millions of devotees during the Mandala-Makaravilakku season (November-January). The unique feature is that it's open to people of all religions and castes.

Pathanamthitta District favorite Wishlist
Guruvayur Sri Krishna Temple

Known as the 'Dwarka of the South', Guruvayur Temple is one of the most important Krishna temples in India. The temple's main deity is Lord Guruvayurappan (Krishna in his child form). The temple has strict dress code requirements and non-Hindus are not permitted inside the main sanctum. The temple elephants, especially the famous 'Gajarajan' Guruvayur Keshavan, are a major attraction.

Guruvayur, Thrissur District favorite Wishlist
Chottanikkara Bhagavathy Temple

This famous temple dedicated to Goddess Bhagavathy (a form of Durga) is renowned for healing mental illnesses and other ailments. The temple has three daily poojas where the deity appears in three different forms - Saraswati in the morning, Lakshmi at noon, and Durga in the evening. The 'Makam Thozhal' festival in February-March attracts large crowds.

Kochi, Ernakulam District favorite Wishlist
Vadakkunnathan Temple

An ancient Shiva temple in Thrissur, Vadakkunnathan Temple is a classic example of Kerala's architectural style with beautiful murals depicting scenes from Mahabharata. The temple is located at the center of Thrissur town and is surrounded by a massive stone wall. The annual Thrissur Pooram festival held here is one of Kerala's most spectacular temple festivals.

Thrissur Town favorite Wishlist
Attukal Bhagavathy Temple

Famous as the 'Women's Sabarimala', Attukal Temple holds the Guinness record for hosting the largest gathering of women for the annual Pongala festival where millions of women cook offerings for the Goddess. The temple architecture is a blend of Kerala and Tamil styles. The deity is believed to be the divine mother who protects her devotees from evil forces.

Thiruvananthapuram favorite Wishlist
Ambalappuzha Sree Krishna Temple

Famous for its 'Palpayasam' (milk porridge offered to the deity), this temple is associated with Lord Krishna in his joyful aspect. The temple has beautiful mural paintings and traditional Kerala architecture. Legend says that the deity here was a gift from Guruvayur temple during a chess game between the local king and Lord Krishna in disguise.

Alappuzha District favorite Wishlist
Ettumanoor Mahadeva Temple

This ancient Shiva temple is famous for its exquisite architecture and the 15-day Ettumanoor Festival held in February-March. The temple is renowned for its mural paintings, especially the one of Nataraja (Dancing Shiva) on the western gopuram. The 'Ezharaponnana' (seven-and-a-half elephants made of gold) procession during the festival is a major attraction.

Kottayam District favorite Wishlist
Vaikom Mahadeva Temple

One of the few temples in Kerala where worship continues round-the-clock, Vaikom Temple is one of Kerala's most important Shiva temples. The temple was a focal point during the Vaikom Satyagraha (1924-25), a movement against caste discrimination. The temple's annual festival in November-December is famous for its cultural programs and elephant procession.

Kottayam District favorite Wishlist
Sree Padmanabhaswamy Temple

This magnificent temple in Thiruvananthapuram is dedicated to Lord Vishnu in his Anantha Shayana posture (reclining on the serpent Ananta). The temple gained worldwide attention when its secret vaults were opened revealing unimaginable treasures. The temple architecture is a blend of Kerala and Dravidian styles with a 100-foot high gopuram.

Thiruvananthapuram favorite Wishlist
Chettikulangara Bhagavathy Temple

Famous for its Kettukazhcha festival held in February-March, this temple showcases massive decorated chariots and structures (up to 80 feet tall) carried by devotees. The temple is dedicated to Goddess Bhagavathy and the festival is considered a visual spectacle with hundreds of participants and thousands of spectators.

Alappuzha District favorite Wishlist
Historical Places
Bekal Fort

Bekal Fort is the largest fort in Kerala, spreading over 40 acres. Built in the 17th century, this historic fort offers breathtaking views of the Arabian Sea from its tall observation towers. The fort's keyhole-shaped architecture and its underground tunnels make it a fascinating historical site. The fort has been featured in several Indian films and is now a popular tourist destination with well-maintained gardens.

Kasaragod District favorite Wishlist
Mattancherry Palace

Also known as the Dutch Palace, this Portuguese-built palace in Kochi was presented to the Raja of Kochi in 1555. The palace is famous for its exquisite Kerala murals depicting scenes from the Ramayana, Mahabharata and Puranic legends. The palace also houses rare examples of traditional Kerala flooring and exhibits of royal costumes, palanquins and weapons from the Cochin royal family.

Kochi favorite Wishlist
Padmanabhapuram Palace

This magnificent wooden palace was the seat of the erstwhile rulers of Travancore. Located in Tamil Nadu but owned and maintained by Kerala government, the palace is a masterpiece of traditional Kerala architecture with intricate wood carvings, beautiful murals and secret underground passages. The palace features the world's largest windows made of jackfruit wood and a clock tower that's over 300 years old but still functioning.

Kanyakumari District (Tamil Nadu) favorite Wishlist
Thalassery Fort

Built by the British East India Company in 1708, this fort was a major military center and the nucleus of Thalassery's development. The square fort with massive walls, secret tunnels to the sea and intricately carved doors reflects European architecture. The fort played a significant role in establishing British power in Malabar region and witnessed several historical events including the 1857 rebellion.

Kannur District favorite Wishlist
Bolghatty Palace

Built by the Dutch in 1744 on Bolghatty Island in Kochi, this is one of the oldest existing Dutch palaces outside Holland. The palace was later extended and became the residence of British governors. Now a heritage hotel, the palace offers beautiful views of the Kochi harbor and retains much of its old world charm with antique furniture and spacious rooms. The palace grounds include a golf course and the island itself is a peaceful retreat from city life.

Kochi favorite Wishlist
Krishnapuram Palace

This 18th century palace built by Marthanda Varma of Travancore is famous for its unique Kerala-style architecture with gabled roofs, narrow corridors and dormer windows. The palace houses the largest mural painting in Kerala - 'Gajendra Moksham' measuring 14x11 feet. The palace museum displays antique sculptures, paintings and artifacts including a Buddha statue from the 10th century.

Alappuzha District favorite Wishlist
Palakkad Fort

Also known as Tipu's Fort, this well-preserved fort was built by Hyder Ali of Mysore in 1766. The fort has witnessed several battles between the Mysore rulers and the British. The fort's massive walls, moat and underground chambers reflect its military significance. Today, the fort houses a sub-jail, a Hanuman temple and a government museum displaying artifacts from the region's history.

Palakkad Town favorite Wishlist
Cheraman Juma Masjid

Believed to be the first mosque in India, built in 629 AD by Malik Bin Dinar, a contemporary of Prophet Muhammad. The mosque's architecture reflects Kerala temple style with traditional lamps and oil containers. The mosque has an ancient oil lamp that's said to be burning continuously for centuries. People of all religions bring oil for the lamp as offering, symbolizing Kerala's communal harmony.

Kodungallur, Thrissur District favorite Wishlist
Paradesi Synagogue

Built in 1568, this is the oldest active synagogue in the Commonwealth of Nations. Located in Kochi's Jew Town, the synagogue features hand-painted Chinese tiles, Belgian chandeliers and an ornate gold pulpit. The synagogue complex includes a museum displaying historical records of the Cochin Jewish community. The clock tower with four faces showing different times is a unique feature.

Kochi favorite Wishlist
St. Francis Church

Built in 1503 by Portuguese friars, this is the oldest European church in India. The church was originally Catholic but became Protestant when the Dutch took over Kochi. Vasco da Gama was originally buried here before his remains were moved to Portugal. The church's simple architecture with wooden pulpit and baptismal platform reflects its historical significance as the first European religious site in India.

Kochi favorite Wishlist
Waterfalls
Athirappilly Waterfalls

Often called the 'Niagara of India', Athirappilly is Kerala's largest waterfall with water cascading down from 80 feet. Located on the Chalakudy River, the falls are surrounded by lush green forests that form part of the Sholayar ranges. The falls are a popular location for film shoots and the surrounding area is rich in biodiversity with several endangered species. The best time to visit is during monsoon when the falls are at their mightiest.

Thrissur District favorite Wishlist
Vazhachal Waterfalls

Located just 5 km from Athirappilly, Vazhachal offers a different but equally mesmerizing waterfall experience. The water cascades over multiple rocky tiers creating a spectacular view. The surrounding dense forests are home to the endangered Hornbill and several other exotic birds making it a paradise for bird watchers. The forest department has set up viewing platforms and walkways for safe viewing of the falls.

Thrissur District favorite Wishlist
Meenmutty Waterfalls

Located in Wayanad, Meenmutty is a three-tiered waterfall with a total height of about 300 meters making it one of Kerala's tallest waterfalls. The falls are accessible via a 2 km trek through dense forests. Each tier offers a different perspective - the first tier falls from about 100 feet into a small pool, the second from about 200 feet, and the third is the most spectacular with water plunging down a near-vertical cliff.

Wayanad District favorite Wishlist
Soochipara Waterfalls

Also known as Sentinel Rock Waterfalls, Soochipara in Wayanad is a three-tiered waterfall with a height of about 200 meters. The name 'Soochipara' means 'Needle Rock' derived from the needle-like shape of the rock face. The falls are surrounded by dense forests and tea plantations. Visitors can enjoy swimming in the natural pool at the base and try rock climbing on the adjacent cliffs.

Wayanad District favorite Wishlist
Thusharagiri Waterfalls

Thusharagiri (meaning 'snow-capped mountains') comprises a series of waterfalls formed by the Chalippuzha River. The main waterfalls are Erattumukku, Mazhavil Chattam and Thumbithullum Para. The mist created by the falls gives the appearance of snow-covered mountains. The area is ideal for trekking with trails leading through spice plantations and tribal villages.

Kozhikode District favorite Wishlist
Palaruvi Waterfalls

Meaning 'Stream of Milk', Palaruvi falls from a height of 300 feet making it one of Kerala's most picturesque waterfalls. Located in the forests near Aryankavu in Kollam district, the waterfall forms a pool at the bottom where bathing is permitted. The surrounding area has several medicinal plants and the water is believed to have therapeutic properties. The best time to visit is immediately after monsoon when the water flow is strong.

Kollam District favorite Wishlist
Chethalayam Waterfalls

One of Wayanad's smaller but equally beautiful waterfalls, Chethalayam is known for its picturesque setting and easy accessibility. The waterfall is seasonal and dries up in summer months. The surrounding forests are rich in wildlife including elephants, deer and various bird species. The waterfall is ideal for those looking for a less crowded alternative to Meenmutty and Soochipara.

Wayanad District favorite Wishlist
Kumbhavurutty Waterfalls

Located in the dense forests of Kollam district, Kumbhavurutty is a multi-step waterfall that requires a short trek through spice plantations to reach. The waterfall is known for its natural infinity pool at the top where visitors can swim while enjoying panoramic views of the surrounding valleys. The area is rich in biodiversity with several rare orchid species.

Kollam District favorite Wishlist
Marari Waterfalls

Hidden deep in the forests of Idukki district, Marari is one of Kerala's lesser-known waterfalls that offers a pristine natural experience. The waterfall cascades down a nearly 200-foot rock face into a large pool below. The area is accessible only by trekking through dense forests and rubber plantations. The waterfall is at its best during the monsoon and post-monsoon months.

Idukki District favorite Wishlist
Valara Waterfalls

Located on the Munnar-Kochi highway, Valara is a chain of waterfalls surrounded by lush green tea gardens and forests. The waterfall is easily accessible from the main road making it a popular stop for travelers. The best time to visit is during monsoon when multiple streams join to create a spectacular cascade. The area is also known for its rich birdlife including several migratory species.
